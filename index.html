    • about me

    My name is David Ben-Gurion, and I was the primary national founder of the State of Israel and the
    first prime minister of Israel. I was born as David Grün in the town of Płońsk, which was then under
    Russian rule in Poland, on 16 October 1886. In 1906, I moved to Palestine, and in 1909, I adopted the
    name Ben-Gurion. Throughout my life, I had a deep passion for Zionism, which led me to become a major
    Zionist leader and the executive head of the World Zionist Organization in 1946. As the head of the Jewish
    Agency from 1935, and later as president of the Jewish Agency Executive, I was the de facto leader of
    the Jewish community in Palestine. I largely led its struggle for an independent Jewish state in British-ruled
    Mandatory Palestine. On 14 May 1948, I formally proclaimed the establishment of the State of Israel and
    was the first to sign the Israeli Declaration of Independence, which I had helped to write I led Israel
    during the 1948 Arab–Israeli War and united the various Jewish militias into the Israel Defense Forces (IDF).
    As a result, I became known as "Israel's founding father". I continued to lead Israel until 1963 with a
    short break in 1954–55. My legacy as a national founder and a prominent Zionist leader lives on in the history
    of Israel and the Jewish people.

    1. Three main diffrences between web 1.0, 2.0 and 3.0

    • web 1.0 is read-only web, web 2.0 is read-write web, web 3.0 is semantic and based on machine learning and AI.

    • web 1.0 is a static and mostly made up of static html pages, web 2.0 is a dynamic and used technologies such
      JavaScript, and APIs to provide real-time interactivity and a more seamless user experience. web 3.0 is an intelligent
      and can interpret data and provide personalized content based on the user's preferences.

    • Web 1.0 is a web of information, while Web 2.0 is a web of people and social interaction, and Web 3.0 is a web of things
      and connected devices. Web3.0 is also expected to be a more decentralized web that is based on blockchain technology and
      is more secure and transparent than previous versions of the web.

    2. use cases for each difference, asume we try to search "vegan restaurant":

    • Use case: A user searches for "vegan restaurant" on a search engine and is presented with a list of static HTML pages that
      provide basic information about vegan restaurants in their area, such as their names, addresses, and phone numbers.

    • Use case: A user searches for "vegan restaurant" on a social media platform like "happy cow" and finds a dynamic webpage that
      not only provides information about vegan restaurants in their area but also includes reviews and ratings from other users who
      have visited these restaurants. The user can also interact with other users by leaving their own reviews and comments.

    • Use case: A user searches for "vegan restaurant" on a personalized web application that uses machine learning and artificial
      intelligence to provide personalized recommendations based on the user's dietary preferences, location, and past restaurant
      history. The user can also use a blockchain-based payment system to pay for their meal, ensuring a more secure and transparent
      transaction.
